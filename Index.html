The Growth of Latino Communities in New York City

Since the 1990s there has been a growth in Latino populations in New York City. The following chart, compares 5 nationalities, Puerto Rican, Dominican, Mexican, Ecuadorian and Colombians from 1990-2000 & 2010-2015.

By: Brenda Leon

According to a study by The Center for Latin American, Caribbean & Latino Studies, between 1990 and 2015 New York City's population increased. Latino annual growth are significantly higher that those of non-Hispanic, whites and blacks. This trend in the growth of Latino community shows that there is a liklelihood that Latinos will become the largest race/ethnic group in New York City.

A closer look at the growth of Latino Communities in each borough of New York City shows us that in the 1990s the largest group of Latinos were Puerto Ricans living in The Bronx, while the second largest group is Dominicans.
A flash foward to 2015, the largest group of Latinos by nationalities, are Puerto Ricans in The Bronx, followed by Puerto Ricans and then Mexicans.
